PrefEval Accepted at ICLR 2025 as Oral

Our paper, Do LLMs Recognize Your Preferences? Evaluating Personalized Preference Following in LLMs, has been accepted as an oral presentation at ICLR 2025.

This work addresses a critical gap in LLM evaluation: how well do large language models recognize and follow individual user preferences? While LLMs have become increasingly capable at general tasks, personalization remains a significant challenge.

We introduce PrefEval, a comprehensive benchmark for evaluating personalized preference following across multiple dimensions. The benchmark includes both explicit preference settings (where users state their preferences directly) and implicit preference settings (where the model must infer preferences from context).

The dataset is publicly available on HuggingFace, and you can find the code on GitHub. Visit the project website for more details.
